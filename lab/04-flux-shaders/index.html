UNIT 04 / 05 - LIVE INSTRUMENT

EIGHT FRAGMENT PROGRAMS,
COMPUTED AS YOU WATCH.

Every tile below is a hand-written GLSL fragment shader running in raw WebGL2 - no rendering libraries, no textures, no video files. Hover a tile to overdrive its clock. Click one to inspect it at full resolution, source included. Specimen 008 is the only one that remembers: press and drag to pour ink into its feedback loop.

SPECIMENS 008  ·  UNIFORMS u_time / u_mouse / u_resolution / u_prev  ·  GL DEPENDENCIES 000

PROTOCOL

One shared animation clock drives every visible tile. Offscreen programs are halted by an intersection observer. Tiles render at reduced density; the full-resolution pass is reserved for the inspector. A frame-time governor watches the rolling average - if it sustains above 22 ms, tile density steps down until it recovers (HUD reads Q AUTO).

INPUT

The pointer streams into every program as u_mouse, smoothed by exponential decay. Hovering a tile overdrives its local clock to ×2.2. 008 FLOW is played, not watched - press and drag to pour ink; your velocity bends the current. Keys 1-8 jump straight to a program; ESC returns to the grid.

SYSTEM

The render core is raw WebGL2 and hand-written GLSL ES 3.00 - zero libraries, zero textures, zero build step. Interface motion alone is delegated to GSAP and Lenis. If it is drawn, it is arithmetic.
